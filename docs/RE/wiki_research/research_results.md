## 1. Game Identity & Overview

**Full Title**: Art of War 2: Global Confederation (Episode 1) / Art of War 2: Liberation of Peru (Episode 2) / Art of War 2: Online (multiplayer expansion)

**Genre**: Real-Time Strategy (RTS)

**Developer**: Gear Games Ltd (also listed as GEAR GAMES GLOBAL FZE on iOS App Store)

**Publisher**: HeroCraft

**Key Description** (from App Store):
> "Art of War 2: Global Confederation" is a combination of dynamic gameplay and tactical depths. This game focuses your attention on nothing but battle action and military base construction. There is no need to mine resources as they are now gained automatically. You can lead an army in campaign mode or play in individual scenarios. Additional maps can also be downloaded from our servers.

**Core Features** (from official listings):
- Beautiful modern 3D-rendered graphics (isometric/top-down perspective)
- 7 campaign missions per episode in different locations
- Big variety of units and upgrades
- Each party has its own weapons and equipment with special characteristics
- Each party has its own battle tactics
- High dynamics of game
- 15 additional custom missions (Global Confederation)
- Resources gained automatically (no manual mining)
- Map editor and custom map support
- Bluetooth multiplayer (Liberation of Peru)
- Online multiplayer (Art of War 2: Online)

## 5. Game Setting & Lore

### Episode 1: Global Confederation (Year 2038)

> "The year 2038. For 20 years, the Earth has been under the rule of the Global Confederation that united and brought under its control all the countries and continents. The mankind entered a new era. Global territory wars that had been waged for a century subsided. The world economy no longer supported the military machine to be ready for action and turned towards the progress. The new scientific horizons on the Earth and in outer space were open.
>
> But peace and quiet in the Confederation is being increasingly disturbed by the outbreaks of rebellions of those who oppose the Senate's policies. The resistance movement is gaining momentum all over the world. The Senate throws the whole reserve of Confederation's scarce peace-keeping forces to suppress the rebellion.
>
> In the east of Peru, near the city of Pucallpa, some signs of rebellious activity have been observed. Prior to that moment, South America was the calmest of regions. Your mission is to crush the revolt in Peru and to capture the leader of the rebellion."

**Player Role**: General of the Confederation, suppressing the revolt in Peru.

### Episode 2: Liberation of Peru (Year 2039)

> "Year 2039. It has been six months since the defeat of the army of the Resistance. All the territory of Peru is under the control of the Confederacy. But the rebels did not surrender. They took refuge in the jungles of the Amazon, have gained strength again and are ready to join the battle to liberate their homeland from bourgeois oppression."

**Player Role**: General José Monteros, leading the Liberation Army of Peru, continuing the work begun by his brother Miguel.

### Confederation Lore (from AOW3 wiki)
The Confederation is described as a representative democracy with a Senate as its primary governing body. They have "the newest technologies and most powerful military vehicles." Their philosophy: "We tried to build a new and perfect world without wars and violence. But today, we must rise in arms against the global threat."

### Resistance Lore (from AOW3 wiki)
The Resistance is described as a liberation army fighting for freedom, justice, and human rights. Their philosophy: "Our strength lies in speed, stealth and surprise. Our weapons are reliable and tested by time."

## 6. Factions

### Global Confederation

**Characteristics**:
- Advanced technology and newest military equipment
- Well-armored and equipped units
- Slower movement speed compared to Resistance
- More expensive units
- Greater firepower per unit
- Defensive doctrine: force enemy to fight on their terms
- T-22 Zeus heavy tank mentioned as a key unit
- Assault infantry armed with M-55 assault rifles

**Tactical Doctrine**: "You cannot always outrun your enemy. Instead, you must force the enemy to fight on your terms, running headfirst into your heavily armored meat grinder." A fully upgraded Confederation army is nearly unstoppable once it reaches its destination.

**Key Advantages**:
- Superior armor and firepower
- Strong defensive capabilities
- Technological superiority
- Better suited for head-on engagements

### Resistance

**Characteristics**:
- Speed, stealth, and surprise tactics
- Reliable, battle-tested weapons
- Faster units but less armored
- Cheaper units (can be produced more quickly)
- Collect resources faster than Confederation
- Guerrilla warfare tactics

**Tactical Doctrine**: Hit-and-run, flanking maneuvers, speed advantage. "Resistance can collect resources MORE FASTER than confed! That's already a HUGE advantage!" (community observation confirmed by Gear Games)

**Key Advantages**:
- Resource gathering speed
- Unit production speed
- Mobility and flanking capability
- Cost efficiency

## 7. Units & Combat Mechanics

### Combat System Overview

The game operates on a **rock-paper-scissors balancing framework** across unit categories. Understanding unit matchups is the core of competitive play.

### Unit Category Matchups

| Unit Category | Strong Against | Weak Against | Tactical Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Infantry (Basic) | Rocket Soldiers, Soft Targets | Vehicles, Flamethrowers | Scout units, early-game pressure, meat-shields |
| Rocket Soldiers | Heavy Tanks, Aviation | Basic Infantry, Anti-Infantry Vehicles | Defensive line anchors, cost-effective anti-armor |
| Light Assault Vehicles | Infantry, Recon Units | Heavy Tanks, Stationary Turrets | Flanking maneuvers, harrying resource lines |
| Heavy Battle Tanks | Light Vehicles, Structures | Rocket Infantry (in cover), Aviation | Main assault force, high health pools |
| Helicopters / Jets | Heavy Tanks, Artillery | Anti-Air Vehicles, Flak Turrets | Tactical bombardment, bypassing geographical walls |
| Artillery | Structures, Stationary Targets | Fast Units, Aviation | Siege warfare, area denial |
| Anti-Air Vehicles | Helicopters, Jets | Ground Units | Air defense |

### Unit Types by Faction

#### Confederation Units
- **Assault Infantry**: Main infantry unit, armed with M-55 assault rifles and standard anti-personnel fragmentation grenades (confirmed for AOW2 and AOW3)
- **Rocket Soldiers**: Anti-armor infantry
- **Light Assault Vehicles**: Fast scout/recon vehicles
- **Heavy Battle Tanks**: Including T-22 Zeus (mentioned in strategy guide)
- **Helicopters/Aviation**: Air units for tactical bombardment
- **Artillery**: Long-range siege units
- **Anti-Air Units**: Specialized anti-air vehicles

#### Resistance Units
- **Infantry**: Faster but less armored than Confederation equivalents
- **Rocket Soldiers**: Anti-armor variant
- **Light Vehicles**: Speed-focused assault vehicles
- **Tanks**: Less armored but more mobile
- **Aviation**: Air support units
- **Artillery**: Mobile artillery pieces
- **Anti-Air Units**: Air defense

### Combat Mechanics Details

- **Direct unit control**: Players can directly control individual units or groups
- **Fog of war**: Present in the game
- **Terrain effects**: Different terrain types (jungle, mountains, rivers) affect gameplay
- **Cover system**: Infantry in cover gains defensive bonuses against certain unit types
- **Unit production**: Units produced from appropriate buildings (barracks for infantry, factories for vehicles)
- **Auto-attack**: Units automatically engage enemies in range

## 9. Resource System

### Key Design Decision: Automatic Resource Generation

Unlike traditional RTS games (StarCraft, Command & Conquer), Art of War 2 does **not require manual resource gathering**. Instead:

> "There is no need to mine resources as they are now gained automatically."

### Resource Types

1. **Credits**: Primary currency, generated automatically by factories/supply centers
2. **Power**: Generated by power plants, used to operate buildings and units

### Resource Generation

- **Factories/Supply Centers** generate credits over time automatically
- **Power Plants** generate power for base operations
- Resources accumulate without player micromanagement
- This design choice allows players to focus entirely on combat and strategy

### Faction Differences in Resource Gathering

- **Resistance** collects resources faster than Confederation (confirmed by Gear Games)
- This gives Resistance a significant economic advantage
- Confederation compensates with stronger individual units

## 13. Technology / Research System

### Research / Upgrade Options

From official descriptions and strategy guides:

- **Unit Armor Upgrades**: Enhance defensive capabilities of all unit types
